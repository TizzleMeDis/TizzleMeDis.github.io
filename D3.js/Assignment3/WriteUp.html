Write Up


For this assignment, all three of us were able to work and communicate together to come up with this interactive graph visualization. When we first got together, we first wanted to come into agreement of which dataset we wanted to use. We then decided to use one of our team members dataset from their assignment #2 which contained house prices of every city in California sold from 2000-2020.

When coming up with the interactive graphic, we all wanted to try to make a geographic map graphic where users can click on the city and it displays information about the house market in that city. However due to the time constraint and us being new to D3, we decided it was not the best idea. After two meetings, we all came up with an alternative to create a bar chart race that displayed the top cities with the highest average price house sold from 2000-2020. As well as this bar chart, we included a histogram that allowed users to select a certain California city and display the average price of houses sold each year from 2000-2020. Once we came to agreement with those two graphics, we discussed how much we were going to filter the dataset. Initially, the dataset had so many cities that it would have taken a long time to filter it out, so we decided to only keep the most well known cities such as San Jose, San Francisco, etc. After making those decisions, we each divided the work between the three of us.

When dividing up the work, we had one person filtering the csv file down to only have a list of the most well known California cities and their average price and highest price house sold by year. We also worked together on developing the bar chart race and histogram. We helped each other with visual coding by researching through Observable. We then had one person create and host the webpage. In total, this whole process took 2 weeks with each of us spending 2-4 hours a day researching how to use D3 to create our visuals. The aspect that took the most time was using D3 to create our visuals because all of us were new to D3. Another aspect that took the longest was creating and hosting a webpage because we were also fairly new to this. Overall, it was a fun and great learning experience with this assignment.
